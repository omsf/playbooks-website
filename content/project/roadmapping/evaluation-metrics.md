title: Evaluation and metrics
what: >
  Simply put, metrics are measurable changes over time. More often than not, these are quantitative measurements that represent the state of a project. Metrics are almost never useful as individual points - they require a baseline and some time to determine health. Choosing metrics that are useful to you is highly relative, however it is a universal best practice to regularly evaluate these metrics for usefulness and guidance.
 - Note: Metrics are not the same as Key Performance Indicators (KPIs). Metrics simply provide insight into the state of a project, while KPIs are goals set at the beginning of a project initiative. 
why: >
  To keep up with roadmap development over an extended period of time, you need to find some “leading indicators” of progress that you can measure and track. Metrics serve as short term trackers of effort towards the outcomes of a funded project.
implementations:
  - desc: “Story points” – an arbitrary unit of time used to measure how complex a task is
    advantages:
      - One story point ~ 30 minutes of effort
      - Large efforts should be broken down into tasks that take no more than half a day to complete
    disadvantages:
      - Don’t overestimate the precision of story points. All estimates will be wrong; some will be more useful than others. Powers of 2 or Fibbonacci numbers can each provide useful measurement precision
  - desc: Usage statistics
    advantages:
      - Github statistics
      - Package downloads
      - Citations
  - desc: Benchmark results (see [Benchmarking playbook]())
