title: Project Roadmap
what: >
  An adaptation of the vision into discrete steps, features, or deliverables, sequenced in time. The project roadmap serves as a long term tracker of time towards the outcomes of a funded project.
why: >
  A project roadmap should include the following components to answer the following questions:
   - Goals: What major features can stakeholders expect in the future? What capabilities will your software provide?
   - Timeline: When should stakeholders expect to gain access to these features? Even if it’s impossible to predict the number of quarters or years the development may take, putting goals in sequence can help convey the scope of the project.
   - Milestones: Are there incremental steps along the way to the major features? How will stakeholders know what progress the project has made?
implementations:
  - desc: Large-scale roadmap (~5 years)
    advantages:
      - Should be developed with scientific stakeholders at the outset of the project
      - Translates the project description into a timeline format
   - desc: Annual roadmaps
     advantages:
      - Project leadership should refer to the large-scale roadmap to produce a list of possible priorities for the year.
      - Project staff may be engaged through a brainstorming process to add to or refine this list.
      - Leadership should consult with external stakeholders (such as an [advisory board]()) for feedback on the feasibility, scientific merit, and value of the items on the list.
      - Staff should estimate the effort required to deliver each of the items on the list, taking into account the scientific feedback received.
      - The list of possible priorities may be presented to the governing body to assign priority ranking.
      - The final ranked list will be approved as a governance decision, allowing this plan to serve as a definitive guide to the desires of the governing body.
