title: Defining and building a developer team
what: >
  Scientific software projects typically rely on a few key positions (besides the Project Manager):
    - Technical Lead: Usually a subject matter expert with strong software engineering skills, the Technical Lead is responsible for all the code that gets written. This typically also includes supervisory responsibilities. In some projects, it may make sense to have two Technical leads:
      - Science Lead: Responsible for designing and conducting any relevant experiments, ensuring scientific accuracy and applicability of code, publishing any scientific papers that come out of the project
      - Infrastructure Lead: Responsible for designing software architecture, ensuring performance and stability of the code base, and leading testing and documentation efforts.
    - Research Software Engineers: Skilled software engineers who understand the needs of the scientific community, RSEs create new code to solve scientific problems. Often PhD computational scientists who loved writing production-quality code more than conducting publication-quality experiments. A burgeoning community of RSEs in Europe are defining the position as a distinct career path, and the US is beginning to catch up.
    - Software Scientist: The Software Scientist expands the capabilities of existing software, either through experimentation or method development. Almost always a PhD computational scientist. Sometimes synonymous with (or indistinguishable from) RSE.
why: >
  In the end, all management comes down to human resource management. Defining positions, hiring, and retaining good employees is the single most important set of tasks in the project, and the only truly essential tasks.
when: Always
implementations:
  - OMSF’s compensation policy defines a single category of developer (usually called “Research Software Engineer”) at three levels of seniority. Typical qualifications and responsibilities are as follows: 
  - desc: RSE: PhD, Master’s +3, or Bachelor’s +5
    advantages:
      - Works under supervision
      - interacting primarily with immediate team members and supervisor.
  - desc: Senior RSE: PhD +3 or Master’s +6
    advantages:
      - Works independently
      - Mentors team members
      - Interacts with the broader community
  - desc: Technical Lead: PhD +5  or Master’s +8
    advantages:
      - Takes responsibility across multiple dimensions of the project
      - supervises team members
      - interacts directly with project governance
      - represents the project in public
