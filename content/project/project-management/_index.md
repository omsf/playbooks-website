---
title: Project Management
layout: topic
---

Once a project is planned, funded, and established, it must be executed. That’s where the Project Manager comes in. The PM takes the vision laid out by project leadership and enacts it. A PM does not need to be an expert in either the scientific field or in software engineering, but having some knowledge and skill in the area can be immensely helpful. The most important skills for a PM are prioritization and delegation, but putting these skills into practice requires a deep intuition for the nature of the problem being solved and the skills and limitations of the other members of the team. The PM must be able to hold onto the vision of the project and keep it in sight of the rest of the team, helping them resist distraction and prevent scope creep. The PM should also be comfortable thinking in possibilities, to make strategic decisions that consider both the present reality of the project and multiple potential futures.
