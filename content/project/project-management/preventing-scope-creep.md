title: Following the roadmap: preventing scope creep
what: >
   Scientific software projects are particularly vulnerable to scope creep. One of the most important functions of Project Management is to manage this process and prevent it from grinding progress to a halt. Each subproject should have its scope clearly defined during the planning process. Subproject scopes should be approved by project leadership. Changes to scope should also be approved by project leadership.
why: >
    Expanding the scope of a project once it is planned can lead to overruns in effort and slow down progress
when: Continuous
implementations:
  - desc: Define the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) and deliver this before expanding the scope any further
  - desc: Must-have (MVP), Nice-to-have, Not in scope
  - desc: MoSCoW -- Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have
